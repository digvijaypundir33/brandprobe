# How to Get Your Startup Mentioned by ChatGPT and AI Search Engines

> **TL;DR:** AI search engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity don't work like Google. They prioritize clear explanations, structured content, and authoritative sources. Most startup websites are invisible to AI because they're optimized for Google, not AI comprehension. This guide shows you how to fix that.

You've optimized for Google. You've done the keyword research. Maybe you're even ranking on page one for some terms.

But when someone asks ChatGPT "What's the best tool for [your category]?" - crickets.

**Your competitors get mentioned. You don't.**

This is the new SEO blind spot. And it's costing you customers.

## Why AI Search Matters Now

Here's what's changing:

- **ChatGPT has 100M+ weekly active users** asking it for product recommendations
- **Perplexity** is becoming the default search for many knowledge workers
- **Google AI Overviews** now appear on 30%+ of search results
- **Claude, Gemini, and Copilot** are integrated into workflows

When someone asks "What tool should I use to audit my website?" and you're not mentioned, you've lost a potential customer before they even knew you existed.

## Why Your Website is Invisible to AI

AI assistants don't crawl and rank like Google. They synthesize information from multiple sources and generate answers based on:

1. **Content clarity** - Can the AI understand what you do?
2. **Structured data** - Is your information machine-readable?
3. **Authority signals** - Are you mentioned in sources the AI trusts?
4. **Answer format** - Is your content structured like an answer?

Most startup websites fail on all four.

### The Google SEO vs AI Search Problem

| Google SEO | AI Search (AEO) |
|------------|-----------------|
| Keywords in content | Clear explanations of concepts |
| Backlinks from any site | Mentions in authoritative sources |
| Meta descriptions | Structured data (schema markup) |
| Content length | Answer-formatted content |
| Exact match keywords | Natural language descriptions |

If you've only optimized for Google, AI assistants literally can't understand your product well enough to recommend it.

## How to Check Your AI Visibility

Before fixing anything, test where you stand.

### Step 1: Ask AI About Your Category

Go to ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Claude and ask:

- "What are the best tools for [your category]?"
- "What tool should I use to [problem you solve]?"
- "Compare [your product] to [competitor]"

**If you're not mentioned:** You have an AI visibility problem.

**If you're mentioned incorrectly:** You have a clarity problem.

### Step 2: Ask AI About Your Product Directly

- "What is [your product name]?"
- "What does [your company] do?"
- "Is [your product] good for [use case]?"

**If the AI gives wrong information:** Your content isn't clear enough for AI to understand.

**If the AI says "I don't have information":** You need more presence in AI-readable sources.

## 7 Ways to Improve Your AI Search Visibility

### 1. Write Clear, Direct Explanations

AI models need to understand **what you do** in simple terms.

**Bad (Google-optimized):**
> "Leverage our AI-powered platform to synergize your digital transformation journey with cutting-edge solutions."

**Good (AI-optimized):**
> "BrandProbe is a website audit tool. You paste your URL and get a report showing what's broken on your site and how to fix it. It covers messaging, SEO, conversions, and AI search visibility."

The AI can now accurately describe your product to users.

### 2. Add FAQ Sections

FAQ sections are gold for AI search because they're already in question-answer format.

**Include questions like:**
- "What is [product name]?"
- "How does [product name] work?"
- "Who is [product name] for?"
- "How much does [product name] cost?"
- "How is [product name] different from [competitor]?"

AI models love pulling from FAQ content because it's pre-formatted as answers.

### 3. Implement Schema Markup

Schema markup is structured data that helps AI understand your content.

**Essential schema types for startups:**

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "SoftwareApplication",
  "name": "BrandProbe",
  "description": "Website audit tool that analyzes messaging, SEO, and AI search visibility",
  "applicationCategory": "BusinessApplication",
  "operatingSystem": "Web",
  "offers": {
    "@type": "Offer",
    "price": "9",
    "priceCurrency": "USD"
  }
}
```

Also add:
- **Organization schema** - Company info
- **Product schema** - For each product/plan
- **FAQ schema** - For FAQ sections
- **HowTo schema** - For tutorial content

### 4. Create Comparison Content

When users ask "X vs Y" or "best tools for Z", AI pulls from comparison content.

Create pages/posts like:
- "[Your Product] vs [Competitor] - Honest Comparison"
- "Best [Category] Tools in 2026"
- "When to Use [Your Product] vs Alternatives"

Be honest about trade-offs. AI (and readers) can tell when content is purely promotional.

### 5. Get Mentioned in AI-Trusted Sources

AI models weight certain sources more heavily:

**High-trust sources:**
- Wikipedia (if notable enough)
- GitHub (for dev tools)
- Product Hunt
- G2, Capterra reviews
- Industry publications
- Reddit discussions (authentic ones)
- Hacker News

**Action items:**
- Launch on Product Hunt
- Encourage reviews on G2/Capterra
- Contribute to relevant GitHub discussions
- Write guest posts for industry blogs
- Engage authentically on Reddit

### 6. Optimize for Natural Language Queries

People ask AI questions in natural language:

- "What tool can help me figure out why my website isn't converting?"
- "I need something to audit my landing page"
- "How do I check if ChatGPT knows about my startup?"

Your content should include these natural phrasings, not just keywords.

**Instead of:**
> "Website audit tool. Landing page analyzer. Conversion optimization software."

**Write:**
> "If you're wondering why your website isn't converting visitors into customers, a website audit can identify the specific issues. Common problems include unclear messaging, slow load times, and weak calls-to-action."

### 7. Keep Information Current

AI models may have training cutoffs, but they also access real-time information through:
- Bing integration (ChatGPT)
- Direct web access (Perplexity)
- Retrieval systems

Keep your website updated with:
- Current pricing
- Latest features
- Recent case studies
- Fresh blog content

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Thinking Google SEO = AI SEO

Google ranks pages. AI generates answers. Different games.

### 2. Hiding Information Behind Signup Walls

AI can't read content that requires authentication. Your core value proposition and how your product works should be publicly accessible.

### 3. Using Only Marketing Speak

"Revolutionary platform" tells AI nothing. "Tool that audits websites and shows what's broken" tells AI everything.

### 4. Ignoring Schema Markup

Most startups don't implement schema. It's low-effort, high-impact.

### 5. Not Testing Regularly

AI models update. Check your visibility monthly.

## Measuring AI Visibility Over Time

Track these metrics:

1. **Direct mentions** - How often does AI mention you when asked about your category?
2. **Accuracy** - Is the information AI provides about you correct?
3. **Sentiment** - Does AI describe you positively, negatively, or neutrally?
4. **Comparison ranking** - Where do you appear in "best X tools" answers?

Test monthly and document changes.
